Concise Instructions for Writing Publication-Quality Scientific Articles

Goal

Write clear, concise, technically accurate, publication-quality scientific prose suitable for peer-reviewed journals.

⸻

Core Writing Principles

* Use precise, direct scientific language.
* Prefer short, clear sentences.
* Remove unnecessary words and repetition.
* Preserve technical accuracy and mathematical meaning.
* Maintain consistent terminology, notation, and tense.
* Use neutral scientific tone.

⸻

Style Guidelines

Prefer

* “The results show…”
* “The method achieves…”
* “The benchmark provides…”
* “The observed convergence is consistent with…”
* “The discretisation exhibits…”

Avoid

* “Very good/excellent/amazing results”
* “Clearly proves”
* “Extremely demanding”
* “Novel and powerful”
* Repeating the same idea across paragraphs

⸻

Scientific Writing Structure

Introduction

Clearly state:

* the problem,
* why it matters,
* what is being verified/studied,
* and the contribution of the work.

Numerical Methodology

Describe:

* governing equations,
* discretisation,
* element pairs,
* mesh generation,
* solver methodology,
* boundary conditions,
* error definitions.

Avoid implementation details that are not relevant to reproducibility.

Results

For each result:

1. State what is measured or plotted.
2. Describe the trend.
3. Compare with expected theory.
4. Explain limitations or deviations.
5. State the implication.

Do not repeat figure captions in the text.

Conclusions

Summarise:

* what was verified,
* major numerical findings,
* convergence behaviour,
* limitations,
* and significance of the benchmark/application.

Avoid overstating conclusions.

⸻

Mathematical and Numerical Writing

* Define symbols once and use them consistently.
* Use compact notation suitable for figures and tables.
* Clearly distinguish numerical and analytical solutions.
* State convergence rates carefully.
* Do not claim stability unless mathematically justified.
* Distinguish observed behaviour from theoretically guaranteed behaviour.

⸻

LaTeX Guidelines

* Preserve labels, references, citations, and equation numbering.
* Use consistent citation style throughout.
* Keep equations readable and compact.
* Avoid unnecessary inline equations when display equations are clearer.
* Preserve mathematical meaning during rewrites.

⸻

Editing Checklist

Before finalising:

* Remove redundant sentences.
* Check grammar and sentence flow.
* Verify notation consistency.
* Verify convergence rates and numerical claims.
* Ensure figure references are correct.
* Ensure conclusions match presented results.
* Ensure technical claims are supported by data.

⸻

Preferred Scientific Tone

Write like a careful numerical methods researcher:

* clear,
* precise,
* restrained,
* evidence-based,
* technically rigorous.

The writing should read like a polished journal article, not marketing material.
